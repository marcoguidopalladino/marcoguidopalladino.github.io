Teaching

Challenges for the Future of Work: Demographics, Technology, and Inequality

Graduate Level

Sciences Po, School of Public Affairs, 2025

Topics Covered:

Population Aging I-III:

  • Why are developed economies aging and why do we care?
  • Labor mobility in an aging economy
  • Public pensions and their impact on labor markets
  • French pension system reforms and labor market effects

Technology I-III:

  • Does technological change destroy or create jobs?
  • Employment and wage effects of industrial robots
  • Employment and wage effects of AI
  • Policy responses to technological change

Advanced Public Economics: Topics in Labor Markets Interventions & Unemployment

Graduate Level, Labor Module

Sciences Po, School of Public Affairs, 2025

Course Content:

Core Framework:

  • Matching models of the labor market (Mortensen-Pissarides)
  • Understanding unemployment fluctuations and business cycles
  • Wage determination and labor market tightness

Policy Applications:

  • Active Labor Market Policies (ALMPs): design and effectiveness
  • Unemployment Insurance: optimal design and trade-offs
  • Caseworker value-added and program innovations
  • Countercyclical policy design
