Why Do Firms Use Fixed-Term Contracts?

with Matteo Sartori and Eliana Viviano

Abstract: This paper investigates why firms use fixed-term contracts and the implications for labor market dynamics. We analyze firm-level data to understand the strategic decisions behind temporary employment arrangements.

Firms and the Gender Wage Gap: A Comparison of Eleven Countries

with Antoine Bertheau, Alexander Hijzen, Astrid Kunze and the LinkEED team. [NBER SI 2025 Talk] -- Draft available soon!

Abstract: We quantify the role of gender-specific firm wage premiums in explaining the private-sector gender gap in hourly wages using a harmonized research design across 11 matched employer-employee datasetsâ€”ten European countries and Washington State, USA. These premiums explain the gender gap when women are less likely to work at high-paying firms (sorting) or receive lower premiums than men within the same firm (pay-setting). We find that firm wage premiums account for a substantial share of variation in gender wage gaps, ranging from 15 to 32 percent.

Dignity by Decree? The Employment and Wage Effects of Restricting Fixed-Term Contracts

with Matteo Sartori and Giuseppe Grasso

Abstract: This paper examines the employment and wage effects of policies that restrict the use of fixed-term contracts. We analyze the impact of regulatory changes on labor market outcomes and worker welfare.
